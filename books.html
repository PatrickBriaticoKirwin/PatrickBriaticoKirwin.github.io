Preface on Thinking

Most of the books I recommend have some piece or idea that helps one think about issues. I come from a math background, which has a lot of useful tools for thinking. When you understand how a proof works, you can apply that mode of thinking to other problems. The difficulty is most other fields are not nearly as well-defined as math. Creative writing, for example, is not going to benefit from proof-like prose. However if you share the same goal as me, searching for Truth, it is a useful mode of reasoning. Most of the following books add some tool or often hidden information that helps understanding the world we live in.

Books

Win Bigly by Scott Adams

Adams introduces many interesting psychological concepts. Persuasion, Framing, and Lenses are the big takeaway ideas.

The Machiavellians by James Burnham

A very good introduction to the Italian school of political science. Each chapter gives at least one useful tool to understand politics from an Elite theory lens. There have been studies that show this lens to be correct, at least in the context of America.
